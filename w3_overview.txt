Week 3 Overview ΓÇô Asthma \+ Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease \(COPD\)

Why Asthma and COPD Are Paired Together

Asthma and COPD are among the most common chronic respiratory diseases encountered in ambulatory care practice\.

Although traditionally taught as separate disease states, many patients demonstrate overlapping characteristics that blur the distinction between asthma and COPD\. These patients often present with:

ΓÇó Persistent respiratory symptoms

ΓÇó Airflow limitation

ΓÇó Recurrent exacerbations

ΓÇó Frequent healthcare utilization

ΓÇó Reduced quality of life

Both asthma and COPD are associated with significant morbidity, and exacerbations contribute to:

ΓÇó Emergency department visits

ΓÇó Hospitalizations

ΓÇó Accelerated lung function decline

ΓÇó Increased healthcare costs

ΓÇó Increased mortality

Modern ambulatory care management focuses on more than symptom control\. Clinicians must simultaneously address:

ΓÇó Exacerbation prevention

ΓÇó Optimization of inhaler therapy

ΓÇó Medication adherence

ΓÇó Inhaler technique

ΓÇó Airway inflammation

ΓÇó Comorbid disease states

ΓÇó Long\-term disease progression

ΓÇó Patient quality of life

This week is designed to help students understand how pulmonary disease management extends beyond simply prescribing inhalers and requires ongoing reassessment, monitoring, and individualized therapeutic decision\-making\.

# Week 4 Overview: Heart Failure \+ Atrial Fibrillation \+ Anticoagulation/Venous Thromboembolism

## Why These Disease States Are Paired

Heart failure, atrial fibrillation, and venous thromboembolism are among the most common cardiovascular conditions managed in ambulatory care and frequently coexist in the same patient\.

Together they significantly increase the risk of:

- Stroke
- Systemic embolism
- Heart failure hospitalization
- Recurrent venous thromboembolism
- Major bleeding complications
- Cardiovascular mortality
- All\-cause mortality

Students must learn to manage these conditions simultaneously rather than in isolation\.

Successful management requires balancing mortality reduction, symptom control, stroke prevention, bleeding risk, medication burden, and patient preferences\.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of Week 4 students should be able to:

- Apply heart failure guidelines to patient\-specific scenarios\.
- Apply atrial fibrillation guidelines to patient\-specific scenarios\.
- Apply anticoagulation and VTE guidelines to patient\-specific scenarios\.
- Develop evidence\-based treatment plans\.
- Identify medication therapy problems\.
- Assess stroke and thromboembolic risk\.
- Assess bleeding risk\.
- Identify medication adherence and access barriers\.
- Perform patient\-centered counseling\.
- Create complete SOAP notes\.
- Develop monitoring and follow\-up plans\.
- Utilize shared decision\-making when benefits and risks must be balanced\.

## Guidelines Used

### Heart Failure

2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure

### Atrial Fibrillation

2023 ACC/AHA/ACCP/HRS Guideline for the Diagnosis and Management of Atrial Fibrillation

### Venous Thromboembolism

2026 ACC/AHA/CHEST VTE Guideline

2021 CHEST Guideline and Expert Panel Report: Antithrombotic Therapy for VTE Disease

## Longitudinal Patients

### Patient A

Foundational case focused on diagnosis and optimization of guideline\-directed medical therapy for heart failure with reduced ejection fraction \(HFrEF\)\.

### Patient B

Adherence and access case focused on atrial fibrillation management, anticoagulation adherence, medication affordability, health literacy, and practical problem solving\.

### Patient C

Higher\-risk patient requiring integration of HFpEF, atrial fibrillation, chronic kidney disease, prior venous thromboembolism, anticoagulation management, and individualized risk\-benefit assessment\.

## Progression of Complexity

### Patient A

Guideline Application

Students focus on identifying evidence\-based therapies and optimizing heart failure treatment\.

### Patient B

Implementation of Therapy

Students focus on overcoming medication access barriers, adherence challenges, and patient concerns\.

### Patient C

Clinical Judgment

Students focus on individualized decision\-making when multiple disease states and competing risks coexist\.

The goal is not simply to know the guidelines\.

The goal is to apply the guidelines to realistic ambulatory care patient encounters\.

## Weekly Workflow

### Monday: Guideline Foundation Day

Students will review:

- Heart failure guideline summary
- Atrial fibrillation guideline summary
- Anticoagulation/VTE guideline summary
- Required readings and resources

The purpose of Monday is to establish the evidence base that will be applied throughout the week\.

### Tuesday: Initial Patient Assessment

Students will:

- Conduct patient interviews
- Review chart information
- Identify medication therapy problems
- Develop initial treatment plans
- Complete SOAP note documentation

Patient focus:

- Patient A: New HFrEF diagnosis
- Patient B: New atrial fibrillation diagnosis
- Patient C: Initial complex cardiovascular assessment

### Wednesday: Follow\-Up Assessment

Students will:

- Evaluate treatment response
- Interpret follow\-up laboratory results
- Assess adherence and adverse effects
- Modify treatment plans as needed
- Complete SOAP note documentation

Patient focus:

- Patient A: GDMT titration and monitoring
- Patient B: Medication access and adherence barriers
- Patient C: Reassessment of anticoagulation and renal function

### Thursday: Advanced Clinical Decision\-Making

Students will:

- Manage complex medication therapy problems
- Address patient\-specific barriers
- Apply guideline recommendations to challenging scenarios
- Complete SOAP note documentation

Patient focus:

- Patient A: Long\-term optimization of HFrEF therapy
- Patient B: Persistent adherence challenges and stroke prevention
- Patient C: Shared decision\-making regarding long\-term anticoagulation

### Friday: Reflection and Journal Club

Students will:

- Reflect on patient care experiences
- Apply literature to clinical practice
- Participate in journal club discussion
- Defend evidence\-based treatment recommendations

### Journal Club

__Atrial Fibrillation \+ Anticoagulation__

__Bleeding Reduction Beyond Factor Xa Inhibition: The AZALEA\-TIMI 71 Trial__

Students will evaluate emerging anticoagulation therapies and discuss how future therapies may influence stroke prevention, bleeding risk, and ambulatory care decision\-making\.

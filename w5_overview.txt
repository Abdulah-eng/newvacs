# Week 5 Overview: Depression \+ Anxiety \+ Tobacco Cessation

## Why These Disease States Are Paired

Depression, anxiety, and tobacco use disorder are among the most common behavioral health conditions managed in ambulatory care and frequently coexist in the same patient\.

Depression and anxiety often occur together and can worsen symptom severity, impair daily functioning, reduce medication adherence, and negatively impact quality of life\.

Patients with behavioral health conditions also use tobacco products at significantly higher rates than the general population\. Many patients report smoking to manage stress, anxiety, or depressive symptoms, creating a cycle that can worsen both physical and mental health outcomes\.

Together these conditions significantly increase the risk of:

ΓÇó Functional impairment

ΓÇó Poor medication adherence

ΓÇó Reduced quality of life

ΓÇó Substance use disorders

ΓÇó Cardiovascular disease

ΓÇó Chronic disease complications

ΓÇó Healthcare utilization

ΓÇó Premature mortality

Students must learn to manage all three conditions simultaneously rather than in isolation\.

## Longitudinal Patients

### Patient A

Foundational case focused on uncomplicated depression, anxiety, and tobacco use disorder management\.

### Patient B

Adherence and access case focused on medication barriers, missed doses, limited counseling access, and practical problem solving\.

### Patient C

Higher\-risk patient requiring management of persistent symptoms, treatment optimization, treatment\-resistant depression concepts, and tobacco dependence\.
